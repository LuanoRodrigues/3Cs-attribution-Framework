Internal ISR compliance checklist (remove before submission)

  • Fit (Analytical Essay): Should synthesize/integrate scholarship, clarify debates, provide new perspectives, and identify new research directions (not typically hypothesis-testing, purely historical, or policy analysis).
  • Word limit: ≤ 15000 words (including references). Track word count in pipeline.
  • Spacing: 1.5 (preferred) or double spaced throughout, excluding figures/tables. (HTML approximates via line-height: 1.5.)
  • Quotations: Short quotes in quotation marks; long quotes indented. Bracket edits; use ellipses for omissions; indicate “emphasis added.”
  • Notes: Footnotes only; no endnotes.
  • Figures/tables: Inline near first mention; tables legible; refer to “supplementary files” (not “online appendixes”).
  • Self-citation blinding: Avoid placeholders like “Author”; write self-citations in third person; quote your own prior text explicitly if directly quoted.
  • Citations: Chicago author–date with page numbers even for paraphrases (e.g., (Spruyt 1994, 63–67)).
  • Chicago style basics: US spelling; serial comma; spell out whole numbers 1–100; define abbreviations at first mention in abstract and again in text; follow ISR reference formatting (see ISR style checklist).
  • Citation diversity: ISR encourages authors to consider evidence on gender citation gaps in IR scholarship while finalizing manuscripts.
  • Figure accessibility (OUP):
    • Do not communicate meaning only through colour; avoid referring to “the red line” as the only cue.
    • Ensure WCAG contrast: non-text objects ≥ 3:1; text ≥ 4.5:1 (normal) / 3:1 (large).
    • Provide alt text alongside each figure caption; alt text should be objective, standalone (avoid “Image of…”), and end with a period.
